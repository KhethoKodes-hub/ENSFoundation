Unseen Scars
in student life

Understanding hidden mental health struggles

Tobhana Wellness

1

Let's start with a question

  • Have you ever felt overwhelmed by academic pressure?
  • Have you ever felt like you don't belong?
  • Have you feared disappointing others?
  • Have you felt exhausted but kept pushing?

Many students silently experience these struggles.

2

Mental health matters

  • Mental health affects how we think
  • How we feel
  • How we handle stress
  • How we relate to others

Healthy mental wellbeing helps students grow, learn and succeed.

3

The hidden side of student life

  • Emotional pressure
  • Identity struggles
  • Financial stress
  • Fear of failure
  • Comparison with others

Many of these struggles remain invisible.

4

Unseen emotional scars

  • Chronic Stress
  • Imposter Syndrome
  • Performance Anxiety
  • Spiritual Guilt
  • Quiet Depression
  • Functional Burnout
5

Chronic Stress

Stress that becomes constant rather than temporary.

  • Mental exhaustion
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Poor sleep
  • Feeling overwhelmed
6

Imposter Syndrome

Feeling like you don't deserve your success.

  • "I don't belong here."
  • "Everyone else is smarter."
  • "Soon people will realise I'm not good enough."
7

Performance Anxiety

  • Panic before exams
  • Fear of presentations
  • Pressure to perform perfectly

Too much pressure can harm confidence and wellbeing.

8

Spiritual Guilt

  • "If my faith was stronger I wouldn't feel this way."
  • "Maybe I'm not praying enough."
  • "Something must be wrong with me."

Mental struggles do not mean weak faith.

9

Quiet Depression

Not all depression is obvious.

  • Students attend lectures
  • Socialize normally
  • Continue performing

But internally feel empty or disconnected.

10

Functional Burnout

Students continue performing but feel emotionally drained.

  • Assignments still submitted
  • Classes still attended
  • Leadership roles maintained
11

Academic Pressure

  • Fear of disappointing lecturers
  • Fear of disappointing parents
  • Fear of repeating a year
  • Grades becoming self-worth
12

The Comparison Trap

  • Comparing grades
  • Comparing opportunities
  • Comparing life progress

Social media can amplify this pressure.

13

The Silent Shame

  • Many struggling students are leaders
  • High achievers hide their difficulties
  • They feel pressure to appear strong
14

Breaking the Stigma

Seeking help is a sign of strength.

  • Counselling
  • Peer support
  • Faith guidance
  • Mental health services
15

Supporting Student Wellbeing

  • Manage time realistically
  • Prioritize rest and sleep
  • Speak openly about struggles
  • Build supportive relationships
  • Seek professional help when needed
16

No Student Should Struggle in Silence

Creating supportive communities can transform lives.
